Chapter 1 - Introduction

Financial Challenges

According to surveys conducted in North America many individuals are facing financial challenges

Today, there are 71 million Baby Boomers, people born between 1946 to 1964. Every day, 10,000 of them turn 65. Many of them are hit hard by financial crisis.

Generation X, born between 1965 to 1980, are middle-aged adults. They are worried about keeping their jobs and paying bills. Many are caught between the burden of taking care of their own family and helping their aging parents

At over 72 million adults, Millennials have surpassed the Baby Boomers as America's largest generation. They are facing even bigger hurdles with the new economy.

Self Security

The traditional pillars of retirement are now in doubt.

Personal Savings

More than half of adult Americans are living paycheck to paycheck. They find themselves in a precarious financial situation where their monthly income just barely covers their essential expenses. This often means that they have little to no savings or financial cushion to fall back on in case of unexpected emergencies or expenses. T

Company Pensions

Company pensions are being canceled or phased out in many organizations. In the U.S. many companies have shifted from traditional defined benefit pension plans to defined contribution plans, such as 401(k)s.

Government Programs

Several American government programs provide security in retirement.

Social Security

ocial Security: Social Security is a federal program that provides monthly benefits to retired workers and their eligible dependents. It is funded through payroll taxes and offers financial support to seniors in retirement.

Medicare: Medicare

Medicare: Medicare is a federal health insurance program for individuals aged 65 and older. It helps cover healthcare expenses, including hospital care, medical services, and prescription drugs.

Supplemental Security Income

Supplemental Security Income (SSI): SSI is a federal program that provides cash assistance to low-income individuals, including the elderly, who have limited income and resources.

Financial Foundation

Like building a house, you should start with a solid financial foundation and build it form the ground up.

Debt Management

Debt management is a crucial aspect of maintaining one's financial well-being. It involves effectively handling and reducing debts, such as credit card balances, loans, and mortgages.

Emergency Fund

Having an emergency fund is a financial safety net that everyone should prioritize. It's a dedicated pool of savings set aside for unexpected expenses, like medical bills, car repairs, or sudden job loss.

Investment

Save and invest for the long run. By putting your money to work in various investment vehicles, such as stocks, bonds, real estate, or retirement accounts, you have the potential to grow your wealth over time.

Protection

Protect in the form of insurance should be a priority in building a solid financial foundation. In case of disability, major illness, or sudden death, savings may not last very long.
